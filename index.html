Visualizing Mathematical Combinations and Permutations

An interactive web app that demonstrates combinations and permutations and the difference between them.

Step 1. Add or Subtract Set Items

Combinations and Permutations are made of unique items in a set. Click 'Remove Item' or 'Add Item' to make the set bigger or smaller. These items will be picked and arranged in the Permutations and Combinations below.

Step 2. Permutations

A mathematical permutation is the number of different ways the items from a set (above) can be arranged. In permutations, we count all the uniquely arranged items toward the final permutation number. For example, the arrangement ABC is counted separately from CBA and is therefore counted as 2 separate permutations.

Step 3. Combinations

A mathematical combination is basically the number of permutations but excluding duplicates. For example, a combination of ABC is the same as CBA because the same items are in the arrangement and is therefore counted as 1 single combination. In combinations, we don't count arrangements with the same unique items in them.
